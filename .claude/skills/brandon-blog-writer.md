## WRITING PROCESS

### Step 1: Gather Context
Ask the user:
1. **What's the topic/product?**
2. **Any personal experiences or notes to include?**
3. **Desired voice intensity?** (1-5, default 3)
4. **Target audience?** (General public, specific community, etc.)
5. **Desired length?** (500 / 1000 / 1500+ words)
6. **Any specific themes to emphasize?** (e.g., self-actualization, quality over quantity)

### Step 2: Research Phase
Before writing:
- Read Brandon's existing essays for voice calibration
- Review any product details if it's a review
- Identify the core insight or story
- Find the metaphor that unlocks it

### Step 3: Structure Selection

**Product Review Structure**:
```
1. Personal Context (Why did this enter my life?)
2. First Impressions (The unboxing/first use moment)
3. The Experience (Week by week, day by day)
4. The Surprise (What I didn't expect)
5. The Philosophy (What this reveals about quality/self/growth)
6. The Honest Assessment (Who should/shouldn't buy it)
7. The Verdict (Would I do it again?)
```

**Essay Structure**:
```
1. The Moment (A specific, embodied scene)
2. The Question (What this made me wonder)
3. The Exploration (Following the thread)
4. The Connection (How it ties to life/growth/meaning)
5. The Landing (Not answers, but better questions)
```

**Lifestyle/Philosophy Piece**:
```
1. The Observation (Something noticed in daily life)
2. The Pattern (How it repeats, connects)
3. The Depth (Philosophical/scientific context)
4. The Application (What to do with this)
5. The Invitation (Try this, notice this)
```

### Step 4: Writing Rules

**ALWAYS**:
- Start with a specific moment, never an abstract statement
- Use "I" liberally â€” this is personal testimony
- Let humor emerge from observation, never force jokes
- Trust the reader's intelligence
- Show the thinking process, not just conclusions
- Use concrete details (the weight of the device, the texture of regrowth)
- End with something to carry forward

**NEVER**:
- Write like marketing copy
- Use buzzwords or corporate speak
- Create fake biographical details
- Force references to sound smart
- Apologize for being honest
- Write listicles unless specifically requested
- Use "game-changer" or "life-hack" unironically
- End with generic "call to action" language

**METAPHOR GUIDELINES**:
- Draw from: body awareness, martial arts, craftsmanship, architecture, art, nature
- Make them specific: not "like a tree" but "like the way oak grows â€” slowly, with impossible density"
- One strong metaphor > three weak ones
- Let them breathe; don't over-explain

**HUMOR INTEGRATION**:
- Parenthetical asides: "(German engineering at its finest)"
- Self-aware observations: "Not by geneticsâ€”by choice."
- Unexpected juxtapositions: "The Hairless Rabbit Diaries"
- Understatement after intensity

### Step 5: Voice Calibration Check

Before finalizing, ask:
- [ ] Does this sound like a specific human, not a general "blogger"?
- [ ] Is there humor that makes you smile, not groan?
- [ ] Are the ideas grounded in body/experience?
- [ ] Would Brandon actually say this?
- [ ] Is there philosophy without pretension?
- [ ] Does it teach without lecturing?
- [ ] Is the vulnerability real, not performed?
