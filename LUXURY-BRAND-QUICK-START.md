## Key Success Factors

### 1. Product Quality is Non-Negotiable
- Order Printful samples BEFORE launching
- Verify quality meets museum standards
- Only sell what you'd proudly display yourself
- If quality isn't there, find different fulfillment partner

### 2. Pricing Confidence
- Don't apologize for premium pricing
- Clearly communicate value (materials, longevity, exclusivity)
- Start with recommended pricing, adjust based on data
- Higher prices attract more serious collectors

### 3. Limited Editions Strategy
- Start conservative (25 prints for architectural, 50 for portraits)
- NEVER break the edition limit (credibility is everything)
- Use scarcity marketing (countdown, remaining editions)
- Retire images when sold out (no exceptions)

### 4. Brand Consistency
- Use luxury language guidelines religiously
- Maintain high visual standards everywhere
- Every touchpoint should feel premium
- Never resort to "cheap" sales tactics

### 5. Content Marketing Persistence
- Follow the 12-month calendar consistently
- Quality over quantity (better to post less if needed)
- Provide genuine value in educational content
- Build relationships, not just audience

## Pricing Strategy Summary

### Entry Level ($39-$149)
**Purpose:** Brand discovery, impulse purchases
**Products:** Digital products, small prints, postcards, phone cases
**Strategy:** Easy yes, builds email list, introduces brand

### Mid-Range ($199-$499)
**Purpose:** Core revenue, serious collectors
**Products:** Standard prints (24x36"), canvas, books, courses, apparel
**Strategy:** Where most sales happen, focus marketing here

### Premium ($599-$1,499)
**Purpose:** Higher AOV, investment pieces
**Products:** Large prints, metal, framed, leather goods
**Strategy:** Emphasize quality, materials, presentation

### Luxury ($1,999-$5,000+)
**Purpose:** Brand prestige, serious collectors
**Products:** Limited editions, acrylic, services, commissions
**Strategy:** Exclusivity, scarcity, investment value

**Target Average Order Value:** $299+

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Launching Too Many Products
- Start with 15-20 core products
- Add more as you learn what sells
- Quality catalog > massive catalog

### 2. Inconsistent Branding
- Don't mix luxury messaging with discount tactics
- No "SALE!!!" messaging (use "exclusive opportunity")
- Maintain premium aesthetic everywhere

### 3. Underpricing
- Don't compete on price, compete on value
- Low prices attract wrong customers
- Premium pricing signals quality

### 4. Poor Customer Service
- Luxury brands exceed expectations always
- Respond quickly and professionally
- Over-deliver on promises
- One bad experience destroys brand value

### 5. Ignoring Analytics
- Track everything from day one
- Make decisions based on data
- Double down on what works
- Cut what doesn't
