## LUXURY LANGUAGE PRINCIPLES

### Words to Use Frequently:

**Quality & Craftsmanship:**
- Museum-quality
- Archival
- Heirloom
- Meticulously
- Precision
- Exceptional
- Exquisite
- Masterfully
- Premium
- Finest

**Exclusivity:**
- Limited edition
- Exclusive
- Rare
- Curated
- Collectible
- Investment piece
- Numbered
- Certified

**Timelessness:**
- Timeless
- Enduring
- Lasting
- Generations
- Permanent
- Classic
- Iconic
- Treasured

**Experience:**
- Discerning
- Sophisticated
- Refined
- Elegant
- Considered
- Thoughtful

### Words to Avoid:

- Cheap, discount, sale (use "exclusive offer" or "special opportunity")
- Basic, standard, normal (use "essential" or describe specifically)
- Mass-produced (use "made to order" or "crafted individually")
- Generic
- Popular (use "sought-after" or "collected")

### Sentence Structure:

**Luxury copy is:**
- Confident without being arrogant
- Specific rather than vague
- Substantiated (explain why something is special)
- Concise but not rushed
- Evocative without purple prose

**Example - Too Generic:**
"Beautiful landscape photograph available now"

**Example - Luxury Positioning:**
"Coastal seascape captured during pre-dawn hours using long-exposure technique. Printed on museum-quality archival paper designed to last 100+ years."
